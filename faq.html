Visitor Assistance

Helping you make the most of your iconic destinations.

General Information

Most landmarks and attractions are best visited early in the morning or during weekdays to avoid heavy crowds and enjoy better photo opportunities.

For popular destinations like Burj Khalifa, London Eye, or the Louvre, we recommend booking tickets online in advance to skip long queues and secure your preferred time slots.

Many attractions offer guided tours with expert narrators that provide historical insights and architectural details. Consider joining these for a richer experience.

Logistics & Comfort

All major landmarks provide accessibility options, including ramps, elevators, and dedicated services for visitors with limited mobility. Check the official site for specific assistance.

Photography is generally allowed at most landmarks, but drone use is restricted. Always verify local rules and respect signage at each site.

Landmarks often have cafes, gift shops, and rest areas nearby. Planning meals and hydration breaks in advance ensures a smoother visit, especially for large sites like Shanghai Tower or the Louvre.

Experiences & Recommendations

At each destination, highlight the unique elements: for example, the observation decks of Burj Khalifa, the Luxor Obelisk at Place de la Concorde, or the architectural torus of the Museum of the Future.

Respect local customs when visiting international landmarks. For instance, dress modestly in religious or historical sites and avoid touching sensitive artifacts.

Combine visits with nearby attractions for a full experience: e.g., Louvre + Eiffel Tower in Paris, Burj Khalifa + Dubai Mall, or the Bund + Yu Garden in Shanghai.
